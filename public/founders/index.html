Vesting & Protection
Equity is earned over time — fair for everyone, including the Founder
📅
4-Year Vesting Schedule

All three co-founders vest their equity over 4 years. This applies equally to the Founder, PM, and BDM — ensuring long-term commitment is rewarded and short-term exits are managed fairly.

🗓
1-Year Cliff

No equity vests during the first 12 months. After the cliff, 25% of each person's total equity vests immediately. The remaining 75% vests in equal monthly instalments over the following 36 months.

🔄
Monthly Vesting After Cliff

After the 1-year cliff, equity vests monthly over months 13–48. This provides predictable and steady ownership accumulation for all three co-founders throughout their tenure.

🔐
Buyback Clause on Unvested Shares

If a co-founder exits before fully vesting, the company retains the right to buy back unvested shares at nominal value. Reverse vesting applies to the Founder too — a signal of good faith and equal accountability to all parties.

BDM Incentive Structure
Rewarding the deals that drive our growth
5%
Closing Bonus on Every Deal
The BDM receives a 5% closing bonus on the total revenue of every new deal they bring in. This bonus is paid directly from that project's revenue before it enters the shared profit pool — it does not come out of anyone else's share.
Worked Example — $5,000 Deal
1. BDM closes a new client deal worth $5,000
2. BDM receives $250 closing bonus (5% of $5,000) from that project's revenue immediately
3. Remaining $4,750 enters the standard revenue waterfall (reserve → costs → designer fee → profit pool)
4. BDM still receives their 30% profit share on top of the closing bonus
Key point: The 5% closing bonus is in addition to the BDM's 30% equity profit share — not a replacement for it.

Reserve Policy
Building financial resilience before optimising for distribution
Phase 1 — Building the Reserve
20%
of all revenue → Reserve
All incoming revenue contributes 20% to the company reserve fund. This phase continues until the reserve reaches a target of 3 months of operating costs, estimated at approximately $4,500–$6,000.
Phase 2 — Reserve Target Reached
10%
of all revenue → Reserve
Once the target balance is reached, the reserve rate drops to 10%. The freed-up 10% difference redistributes to co-founders as additional profit, split by the standard 50/20/30 equity ratio.
🎯 Phase 2 trigger: Reserve balance reaches $4,500–$6,000 (approximately 3× monthly operating costs). At that point the lower 10% reserve rate takes effect automatically and the 10% difference flows directly into co-founder profit distributions.

Next Steps
Three action items to formalise the Sprintix co-founder agreement
1
Agree and Sign Co-Founder Agreement

Review the full co-founder agreement covering equity split, vesting schedule, roles, decision-making rights, and exit clauses. All three co-founders must sign before any client work or revenue is processed.

2
Set Up Company Bank Account with Reserve Separation

Open a dedicated business bank account and establish a separate reserve sub-account or savings pot. Implement the habit of allocating 20% to reserve on every incoming payment from day one of trading.

3
Formalise Designer Fee Rate and BDM Closing Bonus in Writing

Confirm and document the fixed monthly designer fee paid to the Founder, and the 5% closing bonus rate for the BDM. These should be appended to the co-founder agreement or set out in a separate financial schedule signed by all parties.
